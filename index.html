About

I am a Ph.D. student at the School of Computer Science and Technology, Tongji University, advised by Prof. Yi Bin, and a member of the Spatial Intelligence Group (SIG) at Tongji University. My research centers on Embodied AI, working toward the broader vision of spatial intelligence: building agents that perceive, reason about, and act within the physical world. During my undergraduate studies, I worked on the robustness of GUI agents.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in Embodied AI: how agents acquire a usable model of the world and turn it into action. I care about building agents that perceive, reason about, and act robustly in real-world environments, and about the reliability that makes them dependable outside idealized lab settings.

Advisor: Prof. Yi Bin · Accepted to AAAI 2026

Identified that mainstream GUI agents are limited by anomaly-free training and evaluation. Built a dynamic benchmarking framework to model real-world anomalies and diagnosed three core weaknesses in multimodal reasoning: visual grounding precision, reasoning-chain fragility, and task-endpoint detection.
